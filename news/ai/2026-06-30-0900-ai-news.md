# 🗞️ AI Daily Briefing — 2026-06-30

## 🔥 Top Story

> **Anthropic hosts its "AI for Science" livestream today — the capstone of a month-long blitz.** At 10am PST, Anthropic brings together pharma, biotech, and research leaders to showcase Claude-powered scientific workflows. This follows the blockbuster hire of Nobel laureate John Jumper (AlphaFold) from Google DeepMind on June 19, plus new partnerships with the Allen Institute and Howard Hughes Medical Institute. Anthropic is betting that the next major AI unlock isn't bigger language models — it's systematically applying AI to biology, genomics, and the physical sciences. ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/events/the-briefing-ai-for-science-virtual-event)) ([TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318754/20260620/alphafold-nobel-laureate-john-jumper-joins-anthropic-after-nine-years-deepmind.htm))

## 🚀 Model & Research News

- **Gemini 3.5 Pro misses its June launch window, pushed to July.** Google promised GA at I/O in May; it's still in limited Vertex AI preview. The company cites token efficiency issues from early testers. To make matters worse, four senior Gemini researchers announced departures for Anthropic in the last week alone. ([CryptoBriefing](https://cryptobriefing.com/google-delays-gemini-35-pro-launch-to-july-2026/)) ([TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/319318/20260629/gemini-35-pro-cleared-july-launch-fable-5-nears-return-gpt-56-stays-locked.htm))

- **GPT-5.6 Sol remains locked to ~20 government-approved companies.** Four days after OpenAI's June 26 preview, general availability is still "coming weeks." The Trump administration's pre-release national security review process continues to gate the rollout. Sol introduces "ultra" mode with subagent orchestration and a new max reasoning tier. ([OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/previewing-gpt-5-6-sol/)) ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/26/openai-gpt-sol-terra-luna-trump))

- **Fable 5 still offline — day 18 of the export ban.** The U.S. government partially lifted the ban on June 26, restoring Mythos 5 access to ~100 trusted U.S. organizations, but Fable 5 remains blocked globally with no timeline for return. Anthropic and the DOD are reportedly in active legal dispute over the directive's scope. ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/kateoflahertyuk/2026/06/29/us-government-partially-lifts-anthropic-ai-export-ban-what-it-means/))

- **NVIDIA GEAR Lab releases ENPIRE — AI agents now run full robotics research loops on real hardware.** Jim Fan's team (with CMU and UC Berkeley) built a closed-loop framework where coding agents reset scenes, run physical trials, verify outcomes, and rewrite policies autonomously. Achieved 99% pass@8 on contact-rich tasks like seating a GPU into a motherboard. Open-source release planned. ([TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318587/20260617/nvidia-enpire-closes-loop-ai-agents-now-run-robotics-research-real-hardware.htm))

## 🛠️ Tools & Developer Updates

- **GitHub Copilot's first month on metered billing closes today.** Since June 1, developers have reported 10-50x cost increases under the new usage-based credit system. Some burned a month's credits in hours. The backlash has been fierce, with developers threatening to leave for Cursor, Cline, and other alternatives. ([The Register](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/06/02/github-copilot-users-threaten-exit-as-metered-billing-kicks-in/5249826)) ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/30/what-a-joke-github-copilots-new-token-based-billing-spurs-consternation-among-devs/))

- **AlphaEvolve expands into production across genomics, quantum, and infrastructure.** Google DeepMind's Gemini-powered algorithm agent now powers a 30% error reduction in DNA sequencing (DeepConsensus), 10x lower error in quantum circuits on Willow, and is optimizing next-gen TPU designs. Moving from research demo to real-world system. ([Google DeepMind](https://deepmind.google/blog/alphaevolve-impact/))

The broader federal-state tension continues: the White House is pushing to preempt state AI laws while maintaining its own export-control regime that's currently holding Fable 5 hostage.
