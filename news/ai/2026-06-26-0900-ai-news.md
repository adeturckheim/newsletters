# 🗞️ AI Daily Briefing — 2026-06-26

## 🔥 Top Story

> **Google DeepMind is bleeding top talent: Noam Shazeer to OpenAI, John Jumper to Anthropic.** In the span of a single week, Google lost two of its most consequential AI researchers. Shazeer — co-author of "Attention Is All You Need," the paper that started the Transformer era — left to become Lead for Architecture Research at OpenAI. Days later, Nobel laureate John Jumper (AlphaFold) announced he's joining Anthropic after nearly 9 years at DeepMind. Sam Altman called the Shazeer hire "only 10 years in the making." This is the most significant talent exodus from a single lab in recent memory, and it raises real questions about DeepMind's ability to hold the frontier. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/google-gemini-co-lead-noam-shazeer-leaves-for-openai.html)) ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/20/nobel-laureate-john-jumper-is-leaving-deepmind-for-rival-anthropic/)) ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/06/23/google-deepmind-ai-researcher-departures-raise-doubts-about-ability-to-win-the-ai-race-shazeer-jumper-eye-on-ai/))

## 🚀 Model & Research News

- **Anthropic accuses Alibaba of massive distillation attack on Claude.** In a letter to the US Senate Banking Committee, Anthropic claims operators linked to Alibaba's Qwen lab used ~25,000 fake accounts to run 28.8 million exchanges with Claude between April and June 2026, targeting software engineering and agentic reasoning capabilities. Alibaba has not commented. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/24/anthropic-alibaba-distillation-campaign.html)) ([Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/anthropic-claims-that-chinas-alibaba-illicitly-distilled-its-models-from-april-to-june-2026-says-effort-involved-25-000-fake-accounts-and-28-8-million-exchanges-on-claude))
- **Fable 5 still dark — Anthropic confirms zero traffic on day 14 of US export ban.** Anthropic staff publicly debunked viral claims that Fable 5 was accessible again, calling them "categorically false." The Commerce Secretary's congressional response deadline on the suspension is today (June 26). Claude Opus 4.8 remains unaffected and sits at #1 on LMArena. ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access)) ([explainx.ai](https://explainx.ai/blog/is-fable-5-back-2026))
- **GPT-5.6 launch window slips — now likely July.** Prediction markets for a June release collapsed from ~83% to ~18%. OpenAI Chief Scientist Jakub Pachocki internally described it as a "meaningful improvement" over GPT-5.5, with rumored 1.5M token context. Still no official confirmation from OpenAI. ([TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318799/20260621/gpt-56-launch-window-starts-monday-alignment-fix-15m-token-context-inside.htm)) ([Geeky Gadgets](https://www.geeky-gadgets.com/gpt-5-6-june-2026-release/))
- **Grok 4.3 lands on Amazon Bedrock as cheapest US-lab frontier reasoning model.** xAI continues rapid iteration — Grok 4.1 Thinking still holds #1 on LMArena Text at 1483 Elo, while the newer 4.3 prioritizes lower pricing and agentic performance. ([Artificial Analysis](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/xai-launches-grok-4-3-with-improved-agentic-performance-and-lower-pricing)) ([xAI](https://x.ai/news/grok-4-1))
- **Notable research: AgileThinker and AgentHallu.** AgileThinker pairs a cheap reactive head with a deeper planner for real-time reasoning in live environments (trading, robot control). AgentHallu introduces a 693-trajectory benchmark across 7 agent frameworks to test whether models can identify which step in a multi-step run caused a hallucination. ([buildthisnow.com](https://www.buildthisnow.com/blog/guide/mechanics/ai-research-june-2026))

Closing expected H2 2026.
