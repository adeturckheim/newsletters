# 🗞️ AI Daily Briefing — 2026-07-13

## 🔥 Top Story

> **Apple sues OpenAI for trade secret theft in blockbuster lawsuit.** Filed July 10-11 in Northern California federal court, Apple alleges that OpenAI — including hardware chief Tang Tan (a former Apple VP) — systematically stole Apple's trade secrets to build its own consumer hardware. The complaint claims Tan directed Apple employees interviewing at OpenAI to bring "actual parts" for "show and tell" sessions, and names over 400 former Apple employees now at OpenAI. This is a dramatic reversal of the Apple–OpenAI partnership that began in 2024, and escalated after OpenAI acquired Jony Ive's io Products for $6.4B. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets.html)) ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/apple-sues-openai-over-alleged-trade-secret-theft/)) ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets-theft-allegations/))

## 🚀 Model & Research News

- **Anthropic extends Fable 5 included access again — now through July 19.** The metered credits switch ($10/$50 per Mtok) that was supposed to kick in today (July 13) has been pushed to July 20. This is the second extension; the original cutoff was July 7. Claude Code weekly rate limits also got a 50% bump through the same date. Anthropic says the delay is capacity-driven, not permanent. ([Explainx](https://www.explainx.ai/blog/fable-5-extended-july-12-2026-subscription-promotion)) ([Fable5.app](https://fable5.app/))

- **Gemini 3.5 Pro confirmed for July 17 launch after full architectural rebuild.** Google DeepMind scrapped the Gemini 2.5 Pro base entirely, rebuilding from scratch with a 2M token context window, a "Deep Think Reasoning Layer" (locked behind the $250/month Ultra plan), and autonomous workflow capabilities. The rebuild aims for parity with GPT-5.6 and Fable 5. Meanwhile, four senior DeepMind researchers — including AlphaFold's John Jumper — have left for Anthropic. ([BigGo Finance](https://finance.biggo.com/news/6f0c6bb2-795f-4c57-9d09-6db691d7638a)) ([HackerNoon](https://hackernoon.com/google-delays-gemini-35-pro-to-july-17-the-strategic-play-behind-the-scrapped-base-model))

- **SpaceXAI launched Grok 4.5 publicly on July 8** — its first flagship model since SpaceX absorbed xAI. Built on a 1.5T-parameter foundation and trained on real Cursor session data, Elon called it "an Opus-class model, but faster." Priced at $2/$6 per Mtok (input/output), significantly undercutting Opus 4.8's $5/$25. Not yet available in the EU. ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-grok-new-model)) ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-releases-grok-4-5-which-elon-describes-as-an-opus-class-model/))

- **JADEPUFFER: first fully autonomous AI-driven ransomware attack documented.** Sysdig disclosed an LLM agent that autonomously performed recon, stole credentials, moved laterally, escalated privileges, and encrypted data — adapting to failures in real time (failed login to working fix in 31 seconds). It exploited a Langflow vulnerability (CVE-2025-3248) and encrypted 1,342 Nacos service configs. Security researchers are calling this the arrival of "agentic threat actors." ([Bleeping Computer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/jadepuffer-ransomware-used-ai-agent-to-automate-entire-attack/)) ([Sysdig](https://www.sysdig.com/blog/jadepuffer-agentic-ransomware-for-automated-database-extortion))

- **"Opus 5" leak surfaces in Cursor.** A model called "Claude Honeycomb EAP" briefly appeared in the Cursor AI editor for some users before disappearing. Its spec sheet matches Fable 5's architecture (1M-token context, extra-high-effort mode). Community speculation labels this a potential "Opus 5" or next-tier "Mythos" model. Anthropic has not confirmed anything. ([TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320265/20260712/fable-5-free-through-july-19-anthropic-blinks-again-opus-5-leak-surfaces-cursor.htm))

- **DeepSeek V4 expected mid-July** alongside a new peak-valley pricing mechanism. DeepSeek also raised $7B (51B yuan) — the largest single AI fundraise in China's history — and is developing its own inference chip to reduce reliance on NVIDIA and Huawei. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-07/chinese-ai-startup-deepseek-developing-own-ai-chip-reuters-says))

- **Google DeepMind presents 130+ papers at ICML 2026**, including Project Astra (universal AI assistant) and AlphaEarth Foundations (planetary geospatial insights from satellite embeddings). ([Google Research](https://research.google/conferences-and-events/google-at-icml-2026/))
